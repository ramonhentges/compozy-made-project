# ADR-002: Lean Reliability MVP Product Approach

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-04-21

## Context

The transactional outbox initiative has a mature idea brief and an accepted architecture scope in ADR-001. During PRD clarification, the selected business priority was to prevent lost domain events after committed business changes while keeping the MVP small enough to implement quickly.

The first validation workflow should cover all existing Identity domain events. V1 operational visibility should be limited to status fields and logs. The most important readiness signal for expansion beyond Identity is that transient publish failures recover without manual intervention.

## Decision

V1 will use a Lean Reliability MVP product approach.

The PRD will focus on the smallest user-facing capability set that proves reliable delivery for committed Identity domain events: durable event capture, asynchronous delivery with explicit at-least-once semantics, retry recovery for transient failures, and enough status/log visibility for teams to understand event state.

The MVP will defer richer metrics, inspection reports, replay tooling, broader workflow enablement, and event platform features until the reliability path is proven.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Visibility-First MVP

- **Description**: Include basic metrics and an inspection report in the first release alongside durable capture and delivery.
- **Pros**: Improves diagnosis and support confidence from day one.
- **Cons**: Expands scope beyond the selected visibility level and slows the MVP.
- **Why rejected**: The user selected status fields and logs only, with implementation speed as the strongest MVP constraint.

### Alternative 2: Adoption-First MVP

- **Description**: Optimize V1 for downstream workflow adoption through consumer contracts, onboarding guidance, and multiple workflow examples.
- **Pros**: May increase organizational adoption and make the capability easier for teams to consume.
- **Cons**: Diverts effort from proving the core reliability outcome in the first bounded context.
- **Why rejected**: The selected success signal is transient failure recovery, not breadth of adoption.

### Alternative 3: Full Reliability Platform

- **Description**: Deliver durable event capture with advanced replay, inspection, metrics, governance, and multi-workflow enablement in one release.
- **Pros**: Provides a more complete long-term eventing product.
- **Cons**: High scope, slower delivery, and likely overbuild before V1 reliability evidence exists.
- **Why rejected**: It conflicts with the explicit MVP constraint to keep scope small.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Keeps V1 focused on the highest-priority business risk: lost committed domain events.
- Gives teams a reusable reliability path without introducing broad platform scope.
- Produces a clear expansion gate based on transient failure recovery.
- Aligns with existing Identity domain events as the first proof point.

### Negative

- Teams will not get metrics dashboards, inspection reports, or replay tools in V1.
- Broader downstream workflow adoption may wait until after the reliability path is proven.
- Status diagnosis depends on structured state and logs rather than richer operational tooling.

### Risks

- **Insufficient visibility for support**: Mitigate by making status fields and logs explicit acceptance criteria.
- **Slow adoption beyond Identity**: Mitigate by defining Phase 2 expansion criteria in the PRD.
- **MVP perceived as incomplete**: Mitigate by stating non-goals clearly and tying scope to the selected readiness signal.

## Implementation Notes

- Use all existing Identity domain events as the V1 proof surface.
- Treat transient failure recovery as the main release-readiness signal.
- Defer metrics, inspection commands, replay tooling, and adoption packs to later phases.
